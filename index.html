GrowSocial – An Innovative Connection and Collaboration Tool

Imagine if you gave a loom and assortment of yarn to 50 different weavers. It isn’t likely their final tapestries would be the same. Each weaver would create their tapestry with the same basic tools to fit their vision.

Imagine you were building a house to fit the needs of every kind of family: you would want moveable walls, changeable fixtures, and allow every homeowner to furnish and decorate their dwelling. That’s what we’d like to give to the local sustainability community. We’re creating a web platform that will serve in a multitude of ways: to encourage, to educate, to connect and collaborate. We envision a website that will allow local communities to weave the threads of their own tapestry, as their home.

The platform is global but the picture is local: If you were traveling, you could check the site to find local community and get a picture of what they are up to. You’d be able to find people and hear about how they live. You may find inspiration to take home to your own community, spreading the story of how people are thriving.

Points we cover in the application design process:

  • What can the platform do that isn’t already being done on the web?
  • What problems can it solve?
  • What opportunities can it create?
  • Efficiency: an interface easy to understand, intuitive, and organized.
  • Friendly! Support based in community to guide newcomers to familiarity with the platform.

    • There is much internal buzz from the team about all the potential good that GrowSocial can impact:

    • bridging the gap between producers and customers
    • supporting farmers and their products
    • improved household nutrition
    • giving families more access to nutritious food
    • building ecosystem, feeding the hungry
    • a means to trade labor for subsidized produce
    • exposure to markets for marginalized and disadvantaged people

      • also:

      • closing loops to eliminate waste through upcycling, reuse, composting
      • facilitating small business by providing a channel for sales and marketing
      • improving local economy by keeping dollars local
      • improving sustainability through encouragement and education
      • encouraging programs to alleviate malnutrition in food deserts
      • helping mentors and teachers of share their knowledge.
      • raising awareness of sustainable practices
      • creating resilient community by connecting people with local commodities
